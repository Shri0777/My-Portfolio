About

A bit about me.

I'm an undergraduate engineering student at KGISL Institute of Technology with a passion for building software that solves real-world problems. Alongside my academic journey, I work as a freelance web developer, helping businesses establish their online presence and streamline their operations through modern, reliable web applications.

My primary focus is backend development, system design, and creating scalable solutions that are practical, efficient, and easy to maintain. I enjoy turning ideas into complete products by carefully planning the architecture, designing clean APIs, and building applications that prioritize performance, security, and user experience.

Freelancing has given me the opportunity to work on real client projects, understand business requirements, communicate effectively with stakeholders, and deliver software that creates measurable value. Every project is an opportunity to learn, refine my skills, and build solutions that make a meaningful impact.

I believe that great software is not just about writing code — it's about understanding the problem, designing thoughtful solutions, and paying attention to the details that make a product dependable and enjoyable to use.

I'm continuously expanding my knowledge in backend engineering, artificial intelligence, automation, cloud technologies, and modern software architecture. My goal is to build high-quality digital products, collaborate with ambitious businesses, and grow into a software engineer who creates technology that people genuinely rely on.

Outside of development, I enjoy exploring new technologies, documenting what I learn, and challenging myself with projects that push my technical and creative abilities forward.
